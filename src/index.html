Descriptions of the games available

Tic Tac Toe: A classic game for two players, where each player takes turns marking spaces in a 3x3 grid with the symbols (usually "X" and "O"). The player who succeeds in placing three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row wins the game.

Rock Paper Scissors: A simple hand game usually played between two people, where each player simultaneously forms one of three shapes with their hand: rock, paper, or scissors. The winner is determined based on the rules: rock crushes scissors, scissors cuts paper, and paper covers rock.

Guess The Number: A guessing game where the player selects a random number within a specified range, and the other player has to guess the number within a limited number of attempts. The game provides hints (such as "too high" or "too low") to help the player narrow down their guesses.

Quiz Game: A trivia game where players answer questions on various topics, such as history, science, entertainment, and sports. Players earn points for correct answers and compete against each other or the computer to achieve the highest score.

Flappy Bird: An addictive side-scrolling mobile game where players control a bird by tapping the screen to make it flap its wings and navigate through a series of pipes. The goal is to fly as far as possible without colliding with the pipes or hitting the ground.

Snake Game: A classic arcade game where players control a snake that grows longer as it consumes food items placed randomly on the game board. The objective is to guide the snake to eat as much food as possible without colliding with the walls or its own tail.

Tetris: A tile-matching puzzle video game where players manipulate geometric shapes called Tetriminos as they fall down the game field. The goal is to create horizontal lines without gaps by rotating and moving the Tetriminos, causing them to disappear and earn points.

Slide Puzzle: A puzzle game where an image is divided into a grid of squares, with one square missing. Players rearrange the squares by sliding them into the empty space to recreate the original image. The challenge lies in solving the puzzle with the fewest moves possible.
